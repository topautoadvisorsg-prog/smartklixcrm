## Final Execution Model (Authoritative)

**This system is NOT fully autonomous.**
AI does **not** execute final actions on its own.

### The Correct Flow Is:

1. **AI Action Chat**

   * Drafts a proposed action
   * Defines structured input + expected output
   * Submits the proposal to the Review Queue

2. **Master AI Architect**

   * Reviews the draft action
   * Approves or rejects it
   * If approved:

     * Confirms input/output correctness
     * Marks the action as **“Approved – Ready for Execution”**
     * Writes the ledger entry (approved or corrected)

3. **Human Final Executor (Manual Step)**

   * Sees the approved action in a “Ready / Done List”
   * Manually triggers the final execution
   * This is the **only step that actually performs the real action**

---

## Critical Rules (Must Be Enforced in Code)

* ❌ AI agents may **never** trigger the final execution
* ❌ Approval does **not** auto-run anything
* ❌ No background or “helpful” automation after approval
* ✅ AI drafts → Architect approves → **Human executes**
* ✅ Ledger is written at approval time, not execution time

---

## Why This Exists

* Prevents silent or runaway automation
* Ensures human accountability for real-world changes
* Keeps AI focused on being a **high-quality MCP**, not an authority
* Makes every action legally and operationally defensible

This is intentional design, not a temporary limitation.
