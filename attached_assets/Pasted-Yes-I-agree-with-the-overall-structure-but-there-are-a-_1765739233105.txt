Yes, I agree with the overall structure, but there are a few hard contradictions and a few risky assumptions in what you pasted that you should fix before you wire credentials, because they will recreate “nothing happens” and “wrong endpoint” issues.

The big problem you must fix right now
Call End Processing, you still have an intake routing contract violation

In your section “3️⃣ Call End Processing”, you list this under CRM Callback and Logging:

Endpoint: /api/activity-log/write (CRM), /webhook/intake/ai-receptionist

That is not aligned with your locked execution model, and it contradicts what your inventory report said was corrected.

Correct rule:

Call end must write lead intake directly to CRM, for example POST /api/leads/intake

It must not post to any n8n webhook like /webhook/intake/ai-receptionist

So update your document in two places:

In the node list, rename “Send to Lead Intake Flow” to “Write Lead to CRM Intake Endpoint”

In the CRM Callback section, replace /webhook/intake/ai-receptionist with /api/leads/intake and use your CRM base URL

Also, your “Idempotency: CallSid as unique identifier” is correct, keep that.

The second big issue, auth headers are being assumed, not confirmed

Your breakdown repeatedly marks HTTP Request nodes as “✅ Ready” with “Auth header” listed, but earlier audits showed multiple CRM logging nodes missing Authorization headers.

Do not assume they are open.

You need one explicit answer from the CRM developer, and then you apply it everywhere consistently:

Which CRM endpoints require auth

The exact header name and format

Whether Neo8 uses the same token for all tenants or tenant scoped token

Until that is confirmed and implemented, you cannot say “✅ Ready” for any CRM logging node.

Third issue, your Payment Event Processing wording is internally inconsistent

You wrote:

“From Stripe: { event_type, id, payment_intent, charge }”
But you also wrote earlier that the trigger is “CRM Payment Event Webhook, CRM initiated, not Stripe webhook”.

Pick one and lock it in the doc.

If the contract is “CRM calls Neo8 with payment event payload”, then change the wording to:

“From CRM: payment event payload shaped like Stripe events, event_type, id, payment_intent, charge”

If Stripe is ever calling Neo8 directly, then signature verification becomes a required section, and right now it is missing.

Fourth issue, idempotency keys in Outreach are weak

You wrote:

“Idempotency: contactId + actionType + timestamp.”

That is not safe, because timestamp changes each run, so it will not prevent duplicates.

Correct approach under your model:

Use a CRM generated actionId, taskId, or queueItemId in the payload

Neo8 executes once, logs back with that same id

CRM enforces de dupe by actionId

So update your Outreach required payload to include one of:

actionId, taskId, queueItemId, or assistQueueId

If you do not have that yet, this is the exact question to ask the CRM developer:

What stable unique id will be included on every dispatched action so both sides can de dupe, actionId or queueItemId.

Fifth issue, you are still mixing “manual trigger” language

Your outreach trigger is not “manual” in Neo8 terms, it is CRM intent based. That is fine, but your doc should consistently say:

Triggered by CRM action, button click or stage change, and includes defined payload contract
